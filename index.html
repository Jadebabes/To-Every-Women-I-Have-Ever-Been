To Every Woman I Have Ever Been

An Anthology of Shadow, Verse, and Triumph

The Lotus Flower

Upon the asphalt fen where shadows brood, And noxious vapors choked the solitude, There dwelt a bud whom malice sought to bind, In stagnation’s heavy vault confined. Yet didst thou rise, O Lotus of the stream, From turgid depths to catch the solar gleam; Unharmed by rancor, unprofaned by blight, Thy petals drank the uncorrupted light. The iron edicts of a dotard age Sought to immure thee in a gilded cage To clip the pinions of thy soaring thought, And shape thy spirit as the synod taught. But lo! Thy doughty resolve did disdain The craven bondage of a custom vain; With silent might, the ancient bars were rent, Thy sovereign will became thy battlement. No longer captive to the miasma’s spite, Thou stand’st a pharos in the fading night. Wondrous and wild, yet tranquil in thy grace, Time hath no power thy splendor to efface. Thy triumph sings where bitter waters flow: A soul unvanquished by the world below.

The Reader

Beneath the vault where restless mortals chide, And worldly tempests vex the fleeting tide, She seeks a sanctuary dim and deep, Where unremembered kings their vigils keep. Within the narrow cloister of her nook, She yields her spirit to the open book; And with a single breath, the world forsakes, As in her breast a thousand lifetimes wakes. She is the mariner on starless seas, The phantom drifting on an evening breeze; She wears the crown, she bears the exile’s grief, And reaps a century in every leaf. The phantom cares of earth dissolve to dust, Its transient glory and its hollow trust; For in the quiet shadow of her seat, The thrum of empires stays its rushing feet. Here, unperceived by envy’s blinded eye, She sheds the mask and lets the phantom die. No longer bound by custom’s rigid art, She breathes the unfiltered language of the heart. A sovereign exile from the present age, She finds her freedom on the boundless page.

The Fighter

Against the fury of the gathering gale, Where lesser hearts did falter and grow pale, She stood unbowed, a citadel of might, Shielding her purpose through the longest night. Though malice sought to cast its shadow down, And bitter envy schemed to steal her crown, No petty tyrant could her spirit tame, Nor dim the luster of her rising name. Through trial’s fire she forged her destiny, And climbed the apex of her victory; Yet as the plaudits echoed through the skies, No haughty pride unhallowed her calm eyes. For greatness did not steel her heart to stone, Nor make her mock the paths that she had known; She wore her triumph like a mantle light, Most grandly sovereign in her gentle might. The mocking whispers of the cruel throng Were swallowed by the triumph of her song. An icon carved from resilience and grace, Time cannot mar, nor vanity efface; For she who conquered every foe and hill, Remains the noblest, being humble still.
